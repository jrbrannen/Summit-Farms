Important Helpful Study Habits

Where Do You Study?

Many students overlook the importance of where they are attempting to study. Areas with a lot of traffic, loud noises and/or music, or large amounts of people can lead to frequent interruptions. These interruptions can break concentration and focus, hindering the studying process. Other influences around you can seem more Important then what topic your studying to learn. Good studying locations would be in a secluded quite area free from outside distraction or at least keeping outside distractions to a minimum. Some suggested areas would a library, or an empty conference room. Find a area that works for you that will be available to use for a period of time. That way each time you return your brain will automatically associate the area with studying and hopefully prepare itself for a good study session.

Using Methods That Work For You

Another important factor in studying is knowing what works for you. Not everyone learns the same and figuring out what works best for you can take time. Many improve memorization skills by making a game out it refereed to as a mnemonic device, in which important facts are put into an easy to remember words similar to acronyms, rhymes, or imagery. For example the name Roy G. Biv can represent the colors in a rainbow. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet. For some these methods work well but if they don't work for you there are others. Another example would be practice. This can be done through a repetitive behavior such as retaking old assignments or using flash cards with a study buddy. What's important is to find what works for you and to use it.
